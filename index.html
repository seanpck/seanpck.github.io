What is it?

Sense Explorers is a free toolkit developed by Newcastle University, which contains activities and digital resources to involve young people in transforming places and the environment.

The toolkit invites anyone aged 8+ (including those young at heart!) to gather data about problems in their neighbourhood - like air pollution and speeding traffic - and come up with ideas to make it a better place for living, learning and playing. Using their five senses, and some interactive digital tools, young people learn that their neighbourhood is worth looking after - and they have the power to make it better.

Young people will inherit the world we make for them today, but we are not properly preparing them for it, or giving them the tools they need to make our future cities cleaner, healthier and more liveable. Sense Explorers puts them in charge of their own future, by giving them the tools to gather evidence and make a case for improving their neighbourhood. Taking inspiration from the recent climate strikes, the activities and resources allow young people to uncover whether the air is healthy to breathe, cars are going too fast or public spaces should be cleaned up - and design their own solutions to these problems.
The toolkit can be used by anyone who wants to involve children in learning about the environment and making it better, including teachers, youth workers, planners, architects, charities and action groups. It’s completely free to use without any restrictions, and the activities are designed to be adapted for any local setting or project of your choosing.

Sense Explorers comes in four parts: Explore, React, Design and Influence. First, collect data using your own five senses - and a handheld, low-cost digital tool that detects air pollution, dust and noise. Second, think about what this data tells you about your neighbourhood. Third, come up with campaigns and solutions for these problems. Finally, present your ideas to local politicians and decision makers, to promote impact in your local area.
There are a few ways you might use this toolkit.

  • Independent sessions that each last 1-2 hours, based on the Explore, React, Design and Influence elements of Sense Explorers. You can just pick one, or a couple together! Which ones would be most useful for your community?
  • A full day project, ideal for workshops or community days, where you explore each of the elements of Sense Explorers to uncover and respond to problems in your community.
  • A bitesize version of the toolkit, which takes approximately 2 hours to run. This is a great way to have an impact on your community in a short space of time.

The toolkit has strong links with several aspects of school curriculums and youth participation projects. It’s been used in and out of the classroom, in geography projects, school community days and in partnership with local museums.
